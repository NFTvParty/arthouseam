Liner Notes

RAW Q is one of New York's foundational open-format selectors. He doesn't need a theme night… his records are his theme. On this April 10th set for ArthouseAM, he opens with Parliament's Mothership Connection from 1975 and spends the next two hours making the case that hip-hop and funk were always the same conversation.

Three De La Soul cuts, Gang Starr, Nas, Poor Righteous Teachers, Q-Tip, Pete Rock & CL Smooth — the canon, played by someone who helped shape it. Two interludes mark the turns: from hip-hop's golden era into the soul and disco underneath it all, then back again to close with Pete Rock and a Manzel space funk to take you home.

The broadcast aired once, on April 10, 2026. If you had a radio on in New York from 9–11pm, you heard it. If not, the next closest thing is one of 45 hand-numbered cassette copies of the mix — uncut, no interruptions, just 90 minutes of open-format New York heat.
