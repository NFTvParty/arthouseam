Everything I Know About House Music
I Learned From Vinyl

DJStottle
AiredApril 3, 2026  ·  9PM EST
StationWPAT 930 AM  ·  New York
FormatVinyl only  ·  90 min

Liner Notes

I started buying records before I could drive. The first house record I played in a room full of people told me something I’ve been trying to say with music ever since — that the right sequence of sounds at the right moment is as close as I’ve gotten to proof that people belong together.

When we designed arthouseAM, the constraint we kept coming back to was: what if it only happened once? Not as a stunt. Not as a provocation about streaming culture. But because that’s actually how this music was built to be heard — in a room, in a moment, with people who made the choice to show up. A signal going out from a tower that’s been standing in the Meadowlands since 1941, reaching anyone in New York with an AM radio. That’s not nostalgia. That’s an argument about what presence means.

The set I played Friday night I consider in my middle age to be more of a secular House Music sermon than any argument. And that is despite the Jesse Jackson edit in the opening believe it or not. It’s the kind of human expression I fall short of with language, paint brushes, food… pretty much anything else — the kind of expression that only comes from evangelizing on behalf of (and WITH) House Music. And to not only recognize, but educate about its origins in American racism and cultural oppression. Each record I pulled Friday was a privilege: dance music created by musicians, singers and producers from 1970 until just last month. When I’m having a good night behind the decks, I’m merely a servant to House Music’s Holy Ghost. “This record from 1987 belongs in the same sentence as this one from 2019 because they say the same thing differently, and when they’re thirty seconds apart in a mix they make each other true.” Basically speaking in wax tongues.

A few edits I made myself aren’t listed below — not to be precious about it, but they aren’t anything more than temporary DJ tools I made for this specific set. The rest is as honest a tracklist as I can give you.

The mix aired once on April 3rd, 2026. If you were in New York with a radio on that Friday from 9–11pm, you heard it. If you weren’t, you can own the next best thing, 1 of 45 cassette recordings of the mix with no voiceovers or commercials. Just a 90 min mixed House Music tape by myself.

— Stottle  ·  April 2026
