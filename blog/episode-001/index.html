Everything I Know About House Music
I Learned From Vinyl

DJ Stottle
Aired April 3, 2026  ·  9PM EST
Station WPAT 930 AM  ·  New York
Format Vinyl only  ·  90 min

Liner Notes

I started buying records before I could drive. The first house record I played in a room full of people told me something I've been trying to say with music ever since — that the right sequence of sounds at the right moment is as close as I've gotten to proof that people belong together.

When we designed arthouseAM, the constraint we kept coming back to was: what if it only happened once? Not as a stunt. Not as a provocation about streaming culture. But because that's actually how this music was built to be heard — in a room, in a moment, with people who made the choice to show up. A signal going out from a tower that's been standing in the Meadowlands since 1941, reaching anyone in New York with an AM radio. That's not nostalgia. That's an argument about what presence means.

The set I played Friday night is a house music argument. The kind you can't win with words — only with what you put next to what, and when, and at what volume. Each record I pulled is a claim: this belongs next to that. This record from 1987 belongs in the same sentence as this one from 2019. The bassline from a Chicago pressing and the chord from a studio in East London say the same thing differently, and when they're thirty seconds apart in a mix they make each other true.

A few edits I made myself aren't listed below — not to be precious about it, but because they don't have names yet and they aren't finished. The rest is as honest a tracklist as I can give you.

The mix aired once. If you were in New York with a radio on Friday, you heard it. If you weren't, you're holding the next best thing.

— Stottle  ·  April 2026
