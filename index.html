About the Show

Music curated by hand.
Played live.
Heard once.

Arthouse AM is a live radio program Friday nights on WPAT 930 AM in New York — a deliberate counterpoint to the algorithmic age. A weekly communal, ephemeral mix show celebrating vinyl DJs and the power of dance music. No playlists assembled by mathematical formula or payola. No AI-generated recommendations. No second chances to hear what you missed on YouTube.

Every Friday at 9pm, our host Stottle (aka Stottleplex) — with roots stretching back to 1987, having launched the first House Music show on FM radio in Texas at age 16 — will play a monthly set and select a featured monthly guest DJ. His co-pilot for the programming is someone NYC heads already know: the great Raw Q., born and raised in the city with 30+ years of DJ credentials, who will not only bless us with a monthly mix but feature some of the finest 45 DJs in his network on our Friday airwaves.

The mission statement for every artist: “Play a 100 minute mixed set that would make you dance — make a tape with yourself as the single audience.” Our collective of selectors draw from across genres and decades of individual judgment that only time and obsession can produce. It will sound different because it will be different.

Tune in across the New York and New Jersey metropolitan area on AM radio (recommended experience), or stream live from anywhere every Friday night at 9pm EST. Miss it and it’s gone — that’s the point. No digital copies of any mix will be publicly archived, but each will be broken down in a blog post with full track IDs: we’ll ask DJs why they played that record, or whether playing three particular tracks in a row meant something larger subconsciously. Our intent is to shine a light on the art of the mixtape past the technical craft.
